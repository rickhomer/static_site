Jobs at HOMER Energy

The HOMER software has helped tens of thousands of people around the world get clean, renewable energy. We are growing and hiring passionate, dedicated individuals who take initiative and want to be part of a dynamic team. Although we usually prefer to hire locally, we will consider applications from anyone willing to move to Boulder to join our team. If you are a self-starter and a great communicator, we may also, on occasion, hire at a distance and allow you to work from home. Positions marked as "Outsourced" may be filled by any person. All other positions require that you be eligible for work in the United States.

HOMER Energy is located in the center of Boulder at 30th and Walnut, directly across from Twenty Ninth Street, a short distance from the University, and on major bus routes. Our website is located at http://www.homerenergy.com.

Applications are accepted only for currently open positions.

TO APPLY: Send a cover letter explaining why you are a great fit for the position, along with a resume, to jobs@homerenergy.com.
All positions are open until filled.

Director of Web Development

Are you an experienced, creative, professional web expert that would like to head a small but powerful team of creatives and coders delivering software products to every corner of the globe? Are you self-directed and independent minded, while loving to work with a group of equally creative, self-directed individuals to create a world-class customer experience? We are seeking a Director for our web development team to create and manage virtually every aspect of our extensive online presence. This is an exciting position for someone with deep web experience who wants to work specifically in the renewable energy area. This is a key leadership position - you will work at our office in Boulder, Colorado (i.e., no remote workers please).

Duties:
  • Manage and coordinate all company websites (HTML, Ruby on Rails, Wordpress, some PHP, Angular JS)
  • Maintain and and manage PostreSQL database
  • Manage various Linux and Azure servers on various platforms - develop and maintain backups, disaster plans, and upgrade plans
  • Continuous development of new functionality for customer-facing interactive websites
  • Create and maintain documentation for all web-based systems and processes
  • Audit and maintain security systems for all data and multiple websites, using best practices

Minimum qualifications:
  • At least 5 years' experience with websites, in production mode
  • Leader who takes initiative and responsibility
  • Proficient in HTML, CSS, Ruby on Rails, SQL, Javascript, Wordpress
  • API and external integration experience on multiple platforms
  • Fast learner, comfortable with multiple technologies
  • Excellent verbal and written communication skills

Preferred qualifications:
  • 7+ years' experience with production websites
  • Some proficiency with PHP
  • Angular JS
  • PostgreSQL
  • Design sense or background
  • Ecommerce experience, integrating with Authorize.net or similar
  • Linux server setup and management experience
  • Ability to setup and manage Apache web server
  • Experience with SendGrid, MailChimp, or similar, particularly integrating with APIs
  • Experience with Microsoft Azure and Amazon Web Services

Instructional Design Intern

The Instructional Design Intern will aid in the design and development of a comprehensive training and certification program for HOMER's international user base. We seek to ensure quality and consistency in results across their diverse user audience and provide a consistent training experience that takes into consideration a global user group where often English is a second language.

The intern will work under the supervision of an experienced instructional designer. This position does not need to be resident at HOMER Energy.

Responsibilities:
  • Conduct an analysis of training needs through surveys and/or consultation with subject matter experts (HOMER Energy engineers and trainers).
  • Create an instructional design plan that outlines training purpose/goals, learning objectives, learning activities, technology requirements and evaluation methodology.
  • Design, repurpose and develop engaging and interactive software instruction using existing course materials.
  • Design and develop training materials to support a distributed user base.

Qualifications:
  • Master’s degree in instructional design and technology or advanced candidate.
  • Experience with software training.
  • Knowledge of and experience with ADDIE and/or SAM development processes.
  • Expertise in common online learning software e.g. Camtasia, Articulate, Captivate, MS Office.
  • Experience with a variety of learning delivery types (instructor-led, synchronous and asynchronous).
  • Demonstrated ability to collaborate and communicate (both oral and written) effectively with others in the pursuit of common goals.

Compensation:
  • Negotiated.

HOMER Energy LLC is the world’s leading microgrid modeling software company. The HOMER (Hybrid Optimization of Multiple Energy Resources) microgrid software navigates the complexities of building cost effective and reliable microgrids that combine traditionally generated and renewable power, storage, and load management. With over 120,000 users in 193 countries, HOMER is the established global leader for microgrid design optimization and feasibility, and HOMER Energy is a nexus for the microgrid market.
