Microgrid Deployment Forum Takes Shape

HOMER Energy is working with the International Solar Energy Society (ISES) to host its first Microgrid Deployment Forum, in Cancun, Mexico on November 8-9, 2013. Focused on strategies for effectively implementing island and off-grid solutions, day one will systematically examine the key steps and requirements for successful project development and deployment. Day two will consist of an optional in-depth training workshop on use of the HOMER design software for microgrid assessment and design.

The Forum is designed to help developers, project owners, and utilities understand the critical issues involved in deploying hybrid renewable microgrid systems in remote and island environments. The day-two workshop will help HOMER users learn to apply the company’s design software in more advanced applications, as well as serve as an introduction to the tool for new users. Attendees will have an opportunity to network with fellow HOMER users and other microgrid developers from around the world.

The Forum is co-located with the Solar World Congress, November 3-7, 2013, hosted by ISES and the Mexican Solar Energy Association (ANES). SWC typically draws more than 1,000 participants involved in solar energy technologies as well as the hybridization of different renewable energy sources around the world. The event also includes a large commercial exhibition with international companies working in the solar energy field.

For more information on HOMER’s Microgrid Deployment Forum, visit www.microgridconference.com. Those who attend both the Solar World Congress in addition to the HOMER Forum will receive a significant discount. Contact us for more information.

GridSTAR Net Zero Energy Demonstration Project Underway at The Navy Yard

Led by Penn State with support from the U.S. Department of Energy, Commonwealth of Pennsylvania and Philadelphia Industrial Development Corporation (PIDC), the GridSTAR Net Zero Energy Demonstration Structure is the first phase of the GridSTAR Center and will serve as a valuable hub for workforce training, building performance testing, energy management research and “smart” microgrid modernization deployments. The structure is located at The Navy Yard and is slated for completion this summer. [more]

Navigant Releases 2Q 2013 Microgrid Deployment Tracker

As of 2Q 2013, Navigant Research has identified a total of 3,793 MW of total microgrid capacity throughout the world, up from 3,179 MW in the previous tracker update in 4Q 2012. North America is still the world’s leading market for microgrids, with a planned, proposed, and deployed capacity of 2,505 MW, representing 66% of the global capacity. [more]

U.S. Army and Lockheed Martin Commission Microgrid at Ft. Bliss

The microgrid consists of onsite backup generation, a 120 kilowatt solar array, a 300 kilowatt energy storage system, utility grid interconnection and Lockheed Martin's Intelligent Microgrid Control System. The program now enters its demonstration phase, which is slated to continue through July. [more]

Microgrids for Military Bases to Surpass $377 Million in Annual Market Value by 2018

According to a recent report from Navigant Research, U.S. military operations represent the largest consumer of all forms of energy globally. Efforts by the U.S. DOD may be the most crucial push for the overall microgrid market today, especially in terms of control approaches for these smart grid networks based on a bottoms-up distributed model for its operational and tactical deployments of microgrid technology. The Problem with 100% Renewable Energy

In a recent blog post, HOMER CEO Peter Lilienthal argues that up to 80% renewables penetration in a microgrid system makes economic sense. This is the sweet spot for hybrid systems design -- beyond that the need for large-scale energy storage drives up system cost to a point that does not make economic sense. [more]

Top Microgrid Usage Areas and Barriers to Adoption

According to a recent survey of 120 utility executives by Zpryme Research, remote and rural areas are seen as the top usage areas for microgrids, and the top three barriers for adoption are costs, adequate power storage, and interoperability. “With a conservative estimate of $13.4 billion market globally by 2020, microgrids will become an increasingly important factor in the generation, distribution and intake of electricity, gas and water, starting with the BRICS nations,” said Mark Ishac, Managing Director of Zpryme’s Smart Grid Insights Practice.
