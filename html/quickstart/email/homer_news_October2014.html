HOMER Energy Suggests Four Major Microgrid Categories

HOMER Energy recently published a piece suggesting that training, certification, and value standards for microgrids could fit into 4 categories - based first on grid connectivity, and then on the degree of reliability required (for grid-connected systems) and whether or not a utility exists (for isolated systems). One of the great challenges facing the growing microgrid market is the wide range of applications, climates, cultures, resources, and costs associated with a highly dispersed market, and this classification represents a framework for both providers and users to fit themselves within. more
