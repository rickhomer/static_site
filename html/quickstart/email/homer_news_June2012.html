HOMER Energy provides power system expertise to the Carbon War Room

The Carbon War Room, founded by Sir Richard Branson, is an international nonprofit focused on profitable gigaton-scale solutions to climate change. The definitive roadmaps that they create will focus on holistic solutions to the market barriers that reinforce the status quo and prevent capital from flowing to sustainable solutions with compelling returns. Their vision is a world where over $1 trillion invested in climate change solutions is an annual occurrence, not a historic milestone.

The Carbon War Room has engaged HOMER Energy as the power systems expert for their Island Initiative. Islands are a special case for environment and development. They are extremely vulnerable to the devastating impacts of global warming. Ironically, Island Nation States in the tropics have an abundance of renewable resources such as sun and wind but diesel makes up a significant proportion of imports (25% of the total costs of imports for Samoa in 2008). The objective of the Island Initiative is to increase the resilience of island communities by moving away from a diesel dependent economy to a path that uses cheaper and naturally available renewable resources.

Carbon War Room has an agreement with the government of Aruba to produce the first Smart Development Roadmap. Aruba, a small island off the coast of Venezuela, has some of the best wind and solar resources in the world. Earlier this month, HOMER Energy’s CEO Dr. Peter Lilienthal joined Aruba’s Prime Minister Mike Emam, President Jose Maria Figueres of the Carbon War Room, the New America Foundation, and the team of experts to discuss the initiation of Aruba’s Smart Island Strategy. HOMER Energy, as the power systems expert, will model the supply and demand of the power sector, identify cost effective renewable energy projects, and develop the policy, public education and other elements needed  to overcome the challenges of transitioning to renewable energy.
HOMER is a flexible software tool that models a mix of conventional fuels and renewable energy to determine the most cost-effective configuration for each system.
New staff at HOMER Energy
Steve Frank, NSF/ASEE Engineering Innovation Graduate Fellow

This June, HOMER Energy welcomes two new members to the team. Steve Frank and Kathryn Schumacher are joining HOMER this summer as part of the NSF/ASEE Engineering Innovation Fellows Program (EIFP). This program brings together NSF fellows with leading industry partners such as HOMER Energy.

Steve will be examining how HOMER models electrical converters and controllers. Currently, the HOMER model treats controllers and converters as transparent. In reality these components may have slight effects on the economics of the system. Steve will be looking at how we can make these components more explicit, and how they can be accurately and efficiently integrated into the HOMER model.

Kathryn Schumacher, NSF/ASEE Engineering Innovation Graduate Fellow

Kathryn will be working on an upcoming HOMER project, combining forces with the Marine Corps to optimize a version of HOMER for use by the military. Kathryn and Steve are both excited to be joining HOMER energy, and are welcomed to the team.
