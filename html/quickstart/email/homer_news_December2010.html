Dear Friend of HOMER Energy,

I am pleased to announce that HOMER Energy has received recognition from the National Science Foundation and the American Society for Engineering Education in the form of two fellowships. Thanks to the NSF and ASEE, graduate students and recent graduates have an opportunity to work with HOMER Energy.

The first position is a student fellowship for the summer of 2011. The second position is a Postdoctoral Fellowship that will begin in summer 2011 and continue for one year.

The deadline for the student opportunity is January 7, 2011 and is open to graduate students who are current NSF fellows.

The postdoctoral position opens for applications on January 5, 2011.

Please share these exciting openings with students and recent graduates who might benefit from working with us here at HOMER Energy.

Happy holidays,

Peter Lilienthal, CEO
HOMER Energy

Graduate Research Fellow in Hybrid Renewable Powered Micro-Grid Design: January 7 2011 Application Deadline

This graduate student position, open to current NSF fellows, is part of the Engineering Innovation Fellows Program.

Students are encouraged to apply by the Friday, January 7 deadline, for a fellowship with HOMER Energy, located in Boulder, Colorado. One applicant will be chosen to be a Graduate Research Fellow in Hybrid Renewable Powered Micro-Grid Design, with HOMER Energy.

The applicant should be pursuing an advanced degree in Engineering or Operations Research with a focus in renewable energy. Only current fellows of National Science Foundation may apply, and only US Citizens, permanent residents, or those already residing in the United States may apply.

The Research Fellow chosen will be a contributing member of the HOMER team, analyzing and developing hybridized, renewable micro-grids. The successful applicant will also be expected to contribute to our model-enhancement efforts.

Apply now- Deadline is January 7, 2011.
Postdoc opportunity to develop new optimization algorithms for the HOMER model: Opens January 5, 2011

This postdoctoral opportunity - open to recent recipients of a PhD in Engineering, Operations Research, or a related field with a focus on optimization algorithms - is part of the Small Business Postdoctoral Research Diversity Fellowship Program and will focus on developing new optimization algorithms for the Hybrid Optimization Model for Electric Renewables (HOMER).

The position will be open to applications sometime around January 5, we're told. The date is not firm, so be encouraged to check the site that week.

The position is part of our ongoing Small Business Technology Transfer project that is going to transform the HOMER software into an even more powerful tool.

This position is an opportunity for the right person to be deeply involved in the transformation of HOMER. It is expected that the new HOMER algorithm will be developed, coded, and tested within the one-year time frame of this program. The possibility for continuing employment in HOMER Energy LLC, beyond the project, is a distinct possibility.

Review the requirements for the position and read the full description of the fellowship here. On or near Jan. 5, 2011, they will add a link to the bottom of the page that says, "Apply to this research opportunity." Additionally, you can choose the link in the upper, right-hand corner that says, "For Applicants." The date is not firm, so be encouraged to check the site that week.
