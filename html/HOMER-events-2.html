Microgrid Global Summit 2014 - May 20-23, Irvine, California USA

Pre-conference workshop May 20: Microgrid Design and Feasibility Analysis

Overview
This one-day Workshop focuses on evaluating the optimal technology mix for designing hybrid energy systems that operate effectively in grid-tied and off-grid environments. In light of the desire to maximize the use of renewable energy in such microgrids, there is a need to design these systems effectively so as to ensure cost effectiveness and an acceptable business model. To achieve profitability and success it is critical to determine the correct mix of energy storage, wind, solar, generation using traditional fuels, and other resources together in a unified system.

The HOMER software is a unique and widely used design tool for evaluating the feasibility and financial viability of grid-tied and off-grid energy systems that incorporate a range of power resources. The Workshop will start off with a discussion of several case studies in which HOMER has been used to help plan and deploy renewables-centric microgrids in a variety of environments. Emphasis will be on the use of the software to refine the business model for a given deployment and identify the correct mix of energy types in light of specific environmental constraints. After this, the Workshop will delve into a series of hands-on HOMER software training sessions, in which attendees will gain not only a fundamental understanding of the software but also how to use it effectively for more advanced applications.

Microgrid Deployment Workshop 2014 - September 22-23, Barcelona, Spain

Call For Speakers

We cordially invite to you consider submitting a presentation proposal for our next Microgrid Deployment Workshop, currently scheduled for September 22-23, 2014 in Barcelona, Spain.

The event is a two-day program that carefully examines the business case, optimal technology mix, and critical steps for successfully deploying microgrid systems in grid-tied, remote, island, and off-grid environments. The emphasis is on refining the business model as well as effectively planning, designing, and deploying microgrids.

Organized by HOMER Energy, the Workshop will bring together project and technology developers, system owners and utilities, and other energy professionals from across the industry for in-depth information sharing and networking.

The first day consists of a series of panel sessions that systematically analyze the key steps and requirements for deploying hybrid energy microgrid systems, including:

  • Microgrid Market Dynamics, Growth & Barriers
  • Recent Advances in System Controls, Load Management, Power Engineering, & Energy Storage
  • Conceptual Design and Pre-Feasibility Analysis
  • Non-Technical Factors: Local Stakeholder Issues, Permitting, Community Relations, and Cultural Considerations
  • Financing: Off-Taker Creditworthiness, Capital Structures, & Incentives
  • Microgrid Procurement, Construction, & Commissioning
  • Microgrid Operations, Maintenance, & Management Issues

The second day consists of case studies presented by HOMER users, as well as an in-depth, hands-on training workshop focused on the use of HOMER Energy software for the design of such systems.

This Workshop will be similar in scope to our most recent event in Cancun, Mexico on November 2013.
