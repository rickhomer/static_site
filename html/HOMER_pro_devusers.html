Introducing... HOMER Pro

HOMER Energy announces a completely redesigned and updated version of HOMER®, the Hybrid Optimization Model for Multiple Energy Resources, scheduled for release in mid-October, 2014. Originally developed at the National Renewable Energy Laboratory and enhanced and distributed by HOMER Energy, HOMER is the global standard for optimizing microgrid design in all sectors, from village power and island utilities to grid-connected campuses and military bases. HOMER nests three powerful tools in one product, so that engineering and economics work side by side:

  1. Simulation: HOMER simulates the operation of a hybrid microgrid for an entire year, in time steps from one minute to one hour.
  2. Optimization: HOMER examines all possible combinations of system types in a single run, and then sorts the systems according to the optimization variable of choice.
  3. Sensitivity analysis: HOMER lets you ask as many “What if?” questions as you’d like, because you cannot control all aspects of a system, and you cannot know the importance of a particular variable or option without running hundreds or thousands of simulations and comparing the results.

This completely redesigned product has a new look and many new features, including:

  • New design and architecture:
    • Crisp new design and navigation system
    • New library options for managing components, loads, and resources
    • Modular licensing — use only what you need
    • Faster parallel processing architecture
  • Improvements to solar systems:
    • Up to 10 solar arrays
    • Dedicated converters
    • Expanded maximum power point tracker capabilities
    • Concentrating PV and DNI resource
    • Map-based solar data download
  • Improvements to wind:
    • Temperature effect on wind resource
    • Data gap filling in wind; Windographer file import
    • Maintenance scheduling
  • Generator maintenance scheduling
  • Faster and easier ways to simulate common load types
  • Battery minimum state of charge sensitivity
  • Sort and filter results on any column

The core product includes wind, solar (up to 10 arrays), batteries, generators (up to 20), and a single electric load. There are literally hundreds of features — the above list focuses on the changes to HOMER 2.

One of the most significant changes from the user experience perspective is the addition of add-on modules. This feature accomplishes two important goals: (1) It allowed us to keep the price affordable and similar to HOMER 2, while greatly enhancing the product, and (2) it simplifies the interface — customers will not see features that they do not require. The initial modules include:

Hydro: Model run-of river hydro systems. Allows for season stream flow variation, calculates pipe head loss, considers constraints on stream diversion, and many other features.

Biomass: Allows seasonal availability of a biomass resource and gasification ratio and other parameters that describe a gasifier. It also allows a generator that can be co-fired with biogas and another fuel.

Advanced Load: Allows two primary electric loads, so you can perform separate sensitivities on them. They can be on separate buses; one of them can be a critical load and the other can be curtailable. The Advanced Load Module also allows a deferrable load. Unlike a curtailable load, deferrable loads must be met, but HOMER has flexibility around the timing of meeting that load.

Combined Heat and Power: Allows specification of thermal loads that can be met in a number of different ways. The user can specify a percentage of waste heat from the generator that is recoverable, or the user can specify the cost and optimize the capacity of a thermal load controller (also called dump load controller or secondary load controller). The thermal load controller uses energy that would otherwise be dumped or curtailed to meet the thermal load. HOMER also adds a boiler as a default component, so there is never any unserved thermal load.

New add-on modules for hydrogen and unreliable grids are coming January 2015.

Current HOMER 2 licensees will be offered a base version of HOMER Pro to match their HOMER 2 license at no additional cost. We encourage everyone to take advantage of the full-featured 2-week trial version in order to fully explore HOMER Pro’s capabilities. Watch your mailbox for an invitation to download this exciting new product.

HOMER Pro runs on all Windows Operating Systems that are currently supported by Microsoft (Vista, 7, 8, and 8.1).
