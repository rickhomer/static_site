Report Examines Microgrids in Asia Pacific

The market leaders for remote microgrids in Asia Pacific are India, Indonesia, and the Philippines because electrification enhancement is the top priority in these nations. Australia is another top proponent of remote microgrids due to its unique need for power supplies to off-grid or isolated mining sites. Navigant Research forecasts that annual grid-tied and remote microgrid capacity in the nine select countries will grow from 37.0 MW in 2013 to 597.3 MW by 2023 at a compound annual growth rate of 32.1%. more

World Bank Book: How To Promote Mini-Grids in Africa

The World Bank has published a new book called "From The Bottom Up: How Small Power Producers and Mini-Grids Can Deliver Electrification and Renewable Energy in Africa." The book provides a practical guide to government officials and private developers on ground-level regulatory and policy issues that must addressed in order to promote commercially sustainable connected and isolated mini-grids. Successful and unsuccessful real world examples are drawn from Tanzania, Kenya, Sri Lanka, Thailand and Nepal. For a free download of the book, click here.

A Building-Level Microgrid with Integrated PV and Advanced Energy Storage

A recent presentation within the Energy Storage Virtual Summit on April 17 examined an energy storage (ES) deployment at Pualani Manor in Honolulu, HI, in which 50kW/150kWh of ES modules are used for renewable leveling and load flattening. The project is capable of integrated PV and "smart building / integrated islanding" functions for back-up power without utilizing a diesel gen set. California Public Utility Commission Issues Microgrid Report

The report concludes that the role of the electric utility will change, and suggests considering the utility as a distribution system operator, akin to an independent system operator on the transmission side. Additionally, there is a need to develop appropriate standards and requirements to ensure that microgrids interconnect and interact with the distribution grid in a reliable and safe manner. download paper

Clean Energy Hybrid Mini-Grids In Remote Areas - An Investment Opportunity

To achieve a realistic investment opportunity from mini-grid applications in developing countries, a wide range of interrelated issues must be addressed including the assessment of feasibility in target locations, technology needs, policy advice, capacity building, and practical implementation in targeted local communities. This UNEP briefing points out that a bottom-up approach to address customer demand is required. view paper

Energy-Autonomous Smart Microgrids

This presentation, which was part of the Microgrid Rodeo event in February 2014 at the University of Texas at Austin, looks at the concept of dividing the grid into a collection of nearly energy-autonomous sub-systems. The author, Gerard J.M. Smit at the University of Twente Enschede, the Netherlands, outlines what is needed for a series of autonomous microgrids, the stakeholders in such a scenario, and the importance of correct prediction, planning, and control. view slides

HOMER Energy Helps Examine World Interest in Alaska

Alaska's remote micro grid villages, compact road systems and vast renewable energy resources give it first mover advantage in technology development that the rest of the world is beginning to notice. A discussion panel within the Business of Clean Energy in Alaska Conference, May 1-2 in Anchorage explored ways that Alaskans can apply their experiences to a much larger world market that is demanding resilient distributed energy systems. HOMER Energy's CEO, Dr. Peter Lilienthal, participated in the discussion.
