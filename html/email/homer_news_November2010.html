Five Provocative Thoughts on Advanced Energy Storage
Storage has always been a provocative topic with regard to renewable power. Lead acid batteries have long been a mainstay for off-grid systems, but now a variety of new storage technologies are being developed. Interest is increasing in grid-connected micro-grids that can also operate off-grid, either because the grid isn’t reliable enough or as part of a smart grid, demand-response program. This plays perfectly to HOMER’s capabilities. HOMER Energy CEO Peter Lilienthal gave two invited presentations on storage recently – at Battery Power 2010 and at the Utility Wind Integration Group’s Annual Meeting. His key points were:
  • The real challenge with wind and solar occurs when they produce too much power.
  • Baseload vs. peaking is an obsolete distinction; flexibility is the new key attribute.
  • Storage needs multiple value streams to be cost-effective.
  • Storage will not make renewable power act like a conventional generator.
  • Small isolated grids - microgrids - will be the smart grid trailblazers

HOMER Software Tip: Dispatch Strategy

Dispatch strategies can be a complicated topic, but we’ve tried to keep it as simple as possible. The Help file is the first place to look to understand HOMER’s built-in dispatch strategies. Aside from that, we create custom versions of HOMER for users who have special dispatch or other requirements.

The HOMER Help file mentions that load following tends to be optimal in systems with high renewable penetration (wind and PV) and cycle charging tends to be optimal in systems with low renewables penetration. That is just a simplified heuristic or “rule of thumb”.

We’ve created a new Discussion on the HOMER community site where we would love to get feedback from users -such as exceptions to that rule of thumb - or suggestions for improving HOMER’s dispatch strategies. Join it here.

by Peter Lilienthal
