Island Microgrid Development Opportunity
Islands represent an enormous microgrid market, but they come with their own set of development challenges. HOMER Energy is developing a new platform for remote microgrid development in 2013.

HOMER Energy is working with the Carbon War Room to help Necker Island, the Caribbean island owned by Sir Richard Branson, transition away from fossil fuels and increase their use of renewable energy. This effort dovetails with a larger Carbon War Room initiative to engage in sustainable technologies, products and services on islands, starting with a parallel project in Aruba. HOMER Energy is developing a set of conceptual designs for both islands for hybrid renewable-diesel systems to reduce their fuel consumptions and carbon emissions.  

Reznick Think Energy is putting together a request for proposals (RFP) to seek parties that could facilitate renewable energy integration on Necker Island. It would be the responsibility of the winning bidder(s) to design, procure, install, and commission the projects. There may also be opportunities for the winning bidder to participate in ongoing operation and maintenance (O&M) activities.

If you are interested in learning more, please contact Reznick Think Energy directly: rfp@reznickthinkenergy.com. They will ensure that you receive future communications on this RFP. 
HOMER Tip: Inverter Sizing

Many users are surprised when the HOMER software recommends an inverter that is smaller than the peak load. This happens in HOMER systems with a generator.  A system with an inverter that is smaller than the peak load would not be able to always meet the load without a generator. In systems with a generator, however, that generator will meet the peak load more cost-effectively than the inverter. The battery and inverter are best suited to meet smaller loads. 

Unlike inverters, generators have substantial economies of scale, so there is little additional capital cost to having a larger generator.  There is however, a large fuel and maintenance cost to running a generator at low loads.  A system with a large generator and smaller inverter takes advantage of these cost synergies.  There are plenty of exceptions to this general rule, such as systems with DC architecture or very high renewable penetrations. The HOMER software can help you to identify how an inverter can be most cost-effectively used to meet your loads.
