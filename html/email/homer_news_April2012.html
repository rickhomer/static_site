Electrifying Indonesia: 99% Electrification Begins with Local Knowledge & Capacity
John Glassmire, HOMER Engineer, assists a PLN planner who is learning how to use HOMER for island electrification
HOMER trainers recently spent a week in Jakarta, Indonesia assisting Perusahaan Listrik Negara (PLN), the national utility of Indonesia, to begin preparations for a 1000-island electrification program.

The workshop goal was to improve the capacity within PLN to improve rural electrification through renewable technologies. An additional goal was to improve access to electricity for rural poor.

Peter Lilienthal, HOMER Energy CEO, presents the fundamentals of HOMER

Islands are ideal locations for HOMER analysis and application, because they are almost always best served by microgrids.

Indonesia is currently only 65% electrified, and achieving their goal of 99% will require a combination of bolstering grid capacity along with cost-effective generation on smaller islands. HOMER can be a key tool in converting hundreds of existing diesel mini-grids to be hybrid renewable microgrids, and can also be used to find the best systems for those areas with no current power at all.
HOMER is a flexible software tool that models a mix of conventional fuels and renewable energy to determine the most cost-effective configuration for each system.
Renewable Energies for Remote Areas and Islands: Report Available

The International Energy Agency - Renewable Energy Technology Deployment (IEA-RETD) has released an extensive report on policy issues relevant to renewable energy development in remote areas. The goal of the report is "...to provide decision-makers with a better grasp of the technical, economic and energy issues facing remote areas, as well as to provide a menu of policy options available to accelerate renewable energy development in these regions."

The report was developed by a team of specialists from Trama TecnoAmbiental, Meister Consultants, HOMER Energy, and E3 Analytics. The report includes case studies from 12 different areas: Kodiak, Alaska; Newfoundland, Canada; Faroe Islands; Isle of Eigg, Scotland; Galapagos, Ecuador; Coral Bay, Australia; Bonaire; Canary Islands; Miyakojima, Japan; Reunion Island; Antarctica; and Akkan, Morocco.

The 232-page report is available as a free download here.

The report is essential reading for anyone interested in issues in remote renewable energy development
HOMER Tip: The Finances of
Renewables and Storage

One of HOMER's greatest strengths is its financial analysis capability. You can compare the payback time on the purchase of a renewable technology to reduce diesel fuel use, or understand if buying a new battery bank will be profitable. HOMER can help you to determine the present worth, annual worth, return on investment (ROI), internal rate of return (IRR), and payback of these capital investments.  The key to determining financial return is to compare a system to another base case system - in an off-grid system this is frequently a diesel-only electricity supply.

Here’s how to do it. Within the HOMER Optimization Results (i.e. at a specific set of sensitivity values), double-click a system to open the Simulation Results window and select it as the Current System.  From here, click on the Compare… button to open the Compare Economics window.  The Compare Economics window allows you to select a Base case to compare against your Current System.

For the IRR, Simple payback, and Discounted payback metrics to make sense, the Base case system you have selected must have a lower Initial capital value and higher Operating cost than the system you are comparing against (Current system). If these criteria are not met, the IRR, Simple payback, and Discounted payback cannot be calculated and HOMER returns N/A.
