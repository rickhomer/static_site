HOMER Energy's Island Mission
St. Thomas, US Virgin Islands
HOMER Energy CEO Dr. Peter Lilienthal will be a plenary speaker at the upcoming conference on "Creating Climate Wealth." The Washington, DC conference is designed to promote investments in global low carbon, resource-efficient green economies. Organized by Richard Branson, the invitation-only event will convene leading US executives, investors, entrepreneurs, and leaders from the private and public sector. Dr. Lilienthal serves on Richard Branson’s Advisory Group for Island Nations which is part of Branson’s Carbon War Room, a project to leverage the skills of entrepreneurs to tackle climate change.

HOMER Energy is involved in numerous projects with island nations including Bermuda, Anguilla, Haiti, and several island communities in Alaska. Also through an international consortium called "Energy Development for Island Nations"(EDIN) HOMER Energy is helping to diversity the energy resources of the US Virgin Islands. More
You Could Win a SouthWest Windpower Air Breeze wind turbine!
SouthWest Windpower AirBreeze

Wind-diesel systems combine generator sets (gensets) with wind turbine technology to provide electricity - often for remote, off-grid villages. The wind-diesel market is poised for rapid growth: fuel prices have been rising consistently during the last decades, growing environmental concerns are supporting investments in cleaner ways to produce power, and wind-diesel technology has matured through pilot projects and demonstration sites.

XANT turbines have been designed with the specific requirements of the remote, off-grid  market segment in mind. They have fewer parts for maximum reliability, are designed to fit in a single 40-foot container for dramatically lower transport costs, and finally, the airfoils are optimized for low wind speeds.

In order to improve our designs even further, we are seeking feedback from current users of wind-diesel technology. HOMER Tip: TBD

HOMER supports a number of great ways to look at your data, but did
you know that you can customize and export your graphs?  If you
right-click on a graph in the Sensitivity Results, a menu will appear
with a number of useful options:

  • You can copy the graph to your windows clipboard to paste into other applications such as Microsoft Word.
  • You can save the graph as an image on your disk. HOMER supports exports both .png and .emf graphic file types.
  • You can also customize the properties and appearances of your graph. If you select this option, you can adjust the color schemes, the resolution of your graph and change the size of the diamond system markers. You can also change the graph title, the axis labels and displayed units.

by Peter Lilienthal
