IRENA Island Focus for 2012
Dr. Peter Lilienthal at IRENA
HOMER Energy CEO Peter Lilienthal presented at a Pacific region conference sponsored by the International Renewable Energy Agency (IRENA), in partnership with the Government of Australia and the World Bank. The three-day workshop was dedicated to a discussion of  renewable energy in the context of islands, specifically in the Pacific region, which will be a special focus for IRENA in 2012.
 
HOMER software is particularly well-suited to island energy systems. HOMER energy has participated in projects in Bermuda, the US Virgin Islands, Hawaii, Haiti, and many other island locations. Islands face unique energy challenges since they have to use oil for electricity generation. This makes electricity 3-4 times more expensive than in mainland markets.
 
Held in Sydney, Australia, the IRENA workshop hosted some 60 representatives from Pacific island countries and territories and other small island developing states, regional organizations and private industry who attended to discuss strategies for deploying high contributions of renewable energy on islands. The workshop participants presented examples of on-going technology deployment projects and reported their diverse experiences with island renewable energy development. IRENA views the workshop as an important first step toward becoming a one-stop-shop for renewable support.

HOMER Tip: Omitting Configuration Outputs

Have you ever wondered why all of the possible configuration options don't show up in your Optimization Results? By default, HOMER 2 only shows 200 systems for each set of sensitivity values.  These are the category winners and the systems with the lowest Net Present Cost. A higher number may be useful if you want to be able to compare systems that did not have a low NPC or if you want to look more in-depth at a particular high NPC system. In HOMER's early days, computers were unable to handle the full computing capacity of HOMER, and we therefore put in a limit on the number of configurations that were saved. However, these days computers are much more powerful and file sizes can be much larger.

To change the number of stored configurations, click on File >Preferences... (or, alternatively, click on the  button) to open up the Preferences window. There are a number of settings here, but for this tip you'll want to focus on the Number of configurations to store in overall list option. For most modern computers, we recommend you increase this to a high number (at least 5,000), depending on your analysis needs. This setting will apply to new models only -- if you have an existing model, you will need to export the XML and re-import it into a new file after changing the Number of stored configurations setting. (You can import and export the XML from the File menu).

The next next option in the Preference window: Issue Warning if an off-grid system has: is also very useful. It will be discussed in the HOMER tip next month.
