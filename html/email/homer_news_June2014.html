Africa's Largest Self-Sufficient Solar Microgrid Project Created

The island-wide microgrid on Annobon Province, an island off Equatorial Guinea in west central Africa, will be designed to provide enough electricity to handle 100 percent of the island's current energy demand and be the largest self-sufficient solar project on the continent of Africa. The project is a part of Equatorial Guinea's National Economic Development Plan Horizon 2020, which aims to make Equatorial Guinea an "emerging economy" and accelerate its development and democratization by 2020. more

PEER Certification Initiative Launched

Developed by the Perfect Power Institute, a non-profit that aims to apply LEED-style grading to energy microgrids, the new Performance Excellence in Electricity Renewal (PEER) program is designed to implement best practices in system design and set the standard for consumer, municipal, and industrial electrical system performance. more

Energy Storage to Become a Key Part of Microgrids Deployments

A 15% displacement of diesel generator sets in a remote microgrid would result in a payback period of 2-4 years across all energy storage technologies, according to a new report from Navigant Research. The key for storage vendors is to find the right business models, partners, and demonstrate the longevity and robustness required of remote systems. more

Microgrid Alliance Forms, Seeks to Offer Collective Industry Voice

The Microgrid Alliance (MGA) recently formed with the aim of bringing together parties that want to accelerate the growth and development of microgrids. Founding members include General MicroGrids, Landis+Gyr, HOMER Energy, Alstom, ENBALA Power Networks, EarthSpark International, ESTA International, and Green Energy Corp. Among its objectives is the desire to bring a coordinated industry perspective to policy and regulatory discussions at the state level. visit MGA site

Solar Microgrid Architecture Evolution: An Overview

This presentation by David Cohen, Founder and Chairman of Evolution7 Labs, provides an overview of microgrid architectures, including compact networks and hybrid energy systems. Part of the Microgrids Virtual Summit earlier this year, it discusses newer microgrid designs that leverage clusters of intelligent buildings in combination with electric vehicle infrastructure acting as storage and integrated solar systems (solar microgrids) which could be incorporated into many different form factors from residential to commercial to military applications. view audio + PowerPoint recording

Case Study: Technical Meets Non-Technical Factors in Microgrid Design: The Experience of Las Balsas, Esmeraldas, Ecuador

In July 2012, a new 100% photovoltaic microgrid was commissioned in the small remote village of Las Balsas, in Esmeraldas, Ecuador. One of the key aspects of the project has been to seek the sustainability of long-term service. This presentation describes the details of the challenge of preliminary demand studies together with close community engagement to allow for successful engineering, operations and management activities and the sustainability of the service. view slides

UCSD Seeks To Create Dynamic Testing Center for Microgrids

The University of California, San Diego has developed a microgrid platform for showcasing new distributed energy technologies. According to a recent article in greentechmedia, the facility is in a position to shave two years off time-to-market because it is on state property and is not a utility. "That is critical in getting from the lab to market," says center's director, Byron Washom. view article
